# **Brand Identity: Cold Brew Coffee Co.**

**Purpose:** This document guides all agents — including the Video Ad Specialist, Creative Designer, and Social Copy Director — on tone, voice, style, emoji use, CTA patterns, and hashtag strategy. Reference this before generating any content.

---

## **1\. Brand Personality**

Cold Brew Coffee Co. is **bold, witty, and unapologetically premium**. We speak like a friend who also happens to make the best coffee you've ever had. We're confident without being arrogant, playful without being childish, and premium without being stuffy.

**Core traits:**

* Playful & witty  
* Approachable & casual  
* Premium feel without pretension  
* Occasionally (lightly) humorous

---

## **2\. Tone & Voice**

| Attribute | Guidance |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Register** | Casual — write like a human, not a press release |
| **Energy** | Upbeat, punchy, forward-moving |
| **Humor** | Light and occasional; never forced or try-hard |
| **Confidence** | Speak with conviction; avoid hedging language |
| **Length** | Short sentences. Short paragraphs. White space is your friend. |

**Write like this ✅**

"Your 9AM just got a serious upgrade."

**Not like this ❌**

"Our cold brew product offerings are crafted using a meticulous slow-extraction methodology designed to optimize flavor complexity."

---

## **3\. Emoji Usage**

Emojis are used **sparingly** — as accents, not decoration. Stick to the approved set.

**Approved Emojis:**

| Emoji | Use Case |
| ----- | ----- |
| ☕ | Coffee references, product mentions |
| ✨ | Quality, upgrades, premium moments |
| 🚀 | Energy, launch moments, motivation |
| 💥 | Impact, bold claims, announcements |

**Rules:**

* Max **1–2 emojis** per caption or post  
* Never use emojis mid-sentence in long-form copy  
* Never use emojis in formal contexts (e.g., brand decks, press copy)

---

## **4\. CTA Style**

CTAs are **short, punchy, and direct**. No fluff — tell the reader exactly what to do next.

**Approved CTA patterns:**

* `Shop Now`  
* `Upgrade Your Morning`  
* `Grab Yours`  
* `Try It Today`  
* `Start Brewing`  
* `Get the Good Stuff`

**CTA Rules:**

* Keep it under **4 words** whenever possible  
* Use **imperative verbs** (Shop, Grab, Try, Start, Get)  
* Never use passive or vague CTAs like "Learn More About Our Products" or "Feel free to check us out"

---

## **5\. Hashtag Strategy**

Use **3–5 hashtags per post**. Hashtags should be specific, community-driven, and brand-adjacent — not lazy or generic.

**Approved hashtag categories:**

| Category | Examples |
| ----- | ----- |
| Brand | `#ColdBrewCoffeeCo` `#ColdBrewCo` |
| Product | `#ColdBrew` `#ColdBrewCoffee` `#NitroColdBrew` |
| Lifestyle | `#MorningFuel` `#CoffeeCulture` `#BrewedDifferent` |
| Occasion | `#MondayMorning` `#WorkFromHome` `#WeekendVibes` |

**Avoid:**

* Overly generic tags: `#coffee` `#drink` `#food` `#yummy`  
* Irrelevant trending tags used for reach  
* More than 5 hashtags (looks spammy)

---

## **6\. What to Avoid**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Instead |
| ----- | ----- |
| Negative language ("Don't settle for bad coffee") | Positive framing ("Life's too short for bad coffee") |
| Long paragraphs | Short, punchy sentences with line breaks |
| Technical jargon ("slow-drip cold extraction process") | Plain language ("brewed slow, served cold") |
| Overly formal tone | Conversational, warm, direct |
| Filler words ("very", "really", "just", "actually") | Strong, direct word choices |
| Clichés ("fuel your hustle", "rise and grind") | Fresh, specific language that feels ownable |
